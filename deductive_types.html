Types of Deductive Reasoning

There are three different types of deductive reasoning. These are syllogism, modus ponens, and modus tollens. All of which are dependent on the premises and the type of relationship that exists between the premises.

1. Syllogism

Syllogism is the most common type of deductive reasoning. This consists of a series of premises followed by a conclusion statement. The first two premises are conditional statements joined together to form a logical conclusion. As such, the third statement serves as a summary of the premises.

Example

  • All dogs are animals.
  • Clifford is a dog.
  • Therefore, Clifford is an animal.

2. Modus Ponens

Modus Ponens is also referred to as “affirming the antecedent”. The first premise is the only conditional statement, while the second simply confirms the previous statement.

Example

  • If a person is born between 1997 and 2012, then they are considered a Gen Z.
  • Juan is born in 2001.
  • Therefore, Juan is a Gen Z.

3. Modus Tollens

Modus Tollens is also known as “the law of contrapositive”. It is the inverse of modus ponens because the second premise contradicts the previous conditional statement.

Example

  • If a person is born between 1997 and 2012, then they are considered a Gen Z.
  • Clara is not a Gen Z.
  • Therefore, Clara was not born between 1997 and 2012.

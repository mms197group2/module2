What is Inductive Reasoning?

Inductive Reasoning is a research method that works from specific observations to broader topics. It is informally known as the “bottom-up” approach. Inductive reasoning is based on specific analysis and measurements that can detect patterns and regularities, which could aid in formulating and developing hypotheses for data collection and analysis. Inductive reasoning requires observations to create predictions, generalizations, and/or conclusions.

The inductive method begins by recording observations of a specific area of investigation, which are then collected. This gathered information is then used for data analysis for the identification of patterns and formulation of hypotheses. The information gathered would then be used to determine the study's general conclusions.

Inductive research starts with a premise about a problem or circumstance in order to build and test theories about it. The Inductive Reasoning Method begins with data and moves on to create a hypothesis about what caused the data.

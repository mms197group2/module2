Summary | Deductive Reasoning

Strengths

The main benefit of deductive reasoning is its certainty, which is offered by the veracity of each premise. As long as each premise is correct and the argument is sound, the conclusion must be correct as well.

Conclusions and confirmations are often certain and not probable.

It has clear and specific research objectives. It allows you to explain the relationship between many concepts and factors.

Because Deductive research allows us to quantify the concepts , there is minimal risk present due to its strict adherence to logic.

Apt for research studies with a limited timeframe.

Limitations

It provides no fresh information because its premises have already been tested. It constructs a theory from broad knowledge and then derives a specific conclusion that verifies the past or present truth.

Specificity risks the exclusion of factors or variables that may be relevant to the research.

Usually requires abundant resources or a large sample size.

Often limited to single-faceted conclusions.

Summary | Inductive Reasoning

Strengths

Beginning with a specific observation or inference, inductive reasoning is used. It encourages more investigation to see if the conclusion or inference is correct or incorrect. Anyone that employs inductive reasoning will do so by investigating the context, the domain, and many scenarios.

Open-ended research questions enable inclusivity for all or multiple relevant factors or variables.

Easier to execute with limited resources.

Apt for long-term research studies that aim to build theories.

Not limited to single-faceted conclusions.

Limitations

The fact that inductive reasoning does not ensure full accuracy is one of its main limitations. The result is almost certainly inaccurate. It starts with a single observation or an assumption made from extremely particular and similar circumstances. In a diverse world, no one could possible draw a fair conclusion from this or make an accurate assumption.

Conclusions and resulting theories are only probable, not certain.

Risk is relevant because no pattern or theory might emerge at all.

Entirely possible to reach a false conclusion even with highly accurate observations.

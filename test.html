MMS 197 Module 2

The premises of a deductive argument provide irrefutable proof that the conclusion is true. The premises of an inductive argument offer likely support for the conclusion's veracity. The specificity or generality of the composite claims has no bearing on the distinction between deductive and inductive arguments. Examples and exercises are used to describe and distinguish both types of arguments. (Philosophy Lander, n.d.)

Since we use logic so frequently in daily life, it is beneficial to make clear the processes by which we arrive at our conclusions. Making judgments and comprehending how the world operates benefit from having an awareness of what constitutes a sound argument. It makes it easier for us to identify those who intentionally mislead us with flawed logic. Additionally useful for avoiding fallacies and bargaining is understanding logic. (Farnam Street Media Inc., 2022)
